Two types of hydropower

How storage and run-of-river projects differ

A storage (impoundment) project floods a large reservoir to hold water in reserve — allowing operators to generate electricity on demand. A run-of-river project diverts only a portion of the river's natural flow, leaving the landscape largely intact.

Illustration: Both projects sit on the same river. Upstream, a storage dam creates a large reservoir. Downstream, a run-of-river facility diverts only a portion of flow through a penstock and powerhouse before returning water to the river. Courtesy illustration concept from Innavik.
