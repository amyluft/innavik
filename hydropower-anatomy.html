How it works

The anatomy of a run-of-river plant

Unlike traditional impoundment dams, run-of-river facilities divert only a portion of a river's natural flow through a turbine. The rest continues downstream, largely uninterrupted. Click any component to learn more.

Weir
A small structure that creates a tiny pond, just enough to keep the intake submerged and ensure a steady flow into the penstock.
Penstock
A pipe (usually buried) that channels diverted water downhill to the powerhouse, harnessing gravitational energy.
Powerhouse
The heart of the facility — water spins turbines here, driving generators that convert kinetic energy into electricity.
Tailrace
After passing through the turbines, water is returned to the river and continues downstream largely uninterrupted.
Transmission Lines
Generated electricity travels via transmission lines to the community — in Inukjuak's case, replacing almost all diesel generation.
